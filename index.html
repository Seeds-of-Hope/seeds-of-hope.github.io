Seeds of Hope

Discipleship Project

Seeds of Hope is a social and educational initiative that seeks to strengthen vulnerable children with academic, social skill, and techniques that allow them to have the basic tools to overcome the barriers that prevent them from empowering themselves and become leaders in their communities.

The Project seeks that they continue to encourage others to a life of service. This Project is a cooperation of young leaders from different Central American countries with the Hogar del Niño orphanage in the city of Chinandega in Nicaragua. The main goal is to establish lasting relationships with children in the Children's Home using a Christian perspective of companionship, discipleship and collaboration.

About us

Hogar del Niño History

With a gift that very few have, that is teaching and educating, Father Adelino Fossa, of the Jesus Divine Worker Priests Association located in Italy, arrived in Nicaragua 62 years ago to create what is now the Hogar del Niño in the department of Chinandega.

The 89-year-old priest, despite his tired walk, but with great spiritual energy, did not hide his hope and faith for the contribution to the community in the educational part with the orphanage that was founded on January 13, 1961 and in It houses between 130 and 140 children and adolescents of different ages, all under the direction of Father Adelino.

With a low and trembling voice, he said that "the vast majority of those who are in this center are orphaned children, abandoned or who have somehow been victims of some abuse."The majority of the youngsters are from Chinandega, León and Managua.

Hogar del Niño is financed through their own work, since they have a motor rectification workshop, a carpentry workshop, a printing press, a block house, as well as the horticultural farm.

Goals & Expected Results

Goals
  1. Strengthen the knowledge and practice of the English language for children at Hogar del Nino.
  2. Improve the computer system knowledge of the children subject of this project.
  3. Create bonds of friendship and brotherhood among the children subject of the project, collaborators and donors (name of the foundation) of this initiative.
Expected Results
  1. Improved the ability to write and speak English in 20 children at Hogar del Nino who will be the project recipients.
  2. Strengthened the practices and skills of computer use in 30 Children at Hogar del Nino.
  3. Collaborators and children subject of the project consolidate their bonds of friendship and brotherhood with Christian values.

Why do we do it?

One of the Christian values consists in helping others. This means supporting the children of our communities, especially those most in need. This project seeks to encourage others to build lasting relationships through education and the formation Christian values of brotherhood in Jesus Christ, which will ultimately be the basis of our work. In addition, the program aims to create future leaders of their respective communities, leaders dedicated to a life of service and joy following the example of Jesus.
