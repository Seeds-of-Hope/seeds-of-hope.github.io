#SeedsofHope

A discipleship program


“Still other seed fell on good soil. It came up and yielded a crop, a hundred times more than was sown.” - Luke 8:8

Description

One seed planted on fertile soil can grow and make the difference. Such strong believe is the main foundation of the Seeds of Hope - Discipleship Program (SOH); a mentoring and discipleship initiative that seeks to strengthen vulnerable children with academic, social skills, and techniques to overcome the barriers of their current socioeconomical situation by growing in a relationship of the message of Christ. We envision to plant the seeds to create of leaders that encourage others to a life of service in continuous cycle of encouragement.

One of our main development axes is “Christian Life” through the project coordination of Living the Scriptures which is an essential part of every activity we develop under Seeds of Hope. For this reason, the core value of the program is discipleship. Discipleship is teaching biblical precepts while modeling and guiding others toward living righteously as followers of Jesus Christ. This should be a cyclical process—meaning once we are discipled, we are to disciple others, and so on. One of the most important characteristics of being a disciple (a student or pupil), is to develop an intimate relationship with God through Christ rather than just learning about Him. The Christian structure of the program is developed in a way that the children put in practice the biblical lessons they learn from our team. The main goal is to establish lasting relationships with children using a Christian perspective of companionship, discipleship, and collaboration.

Organizational Structure

The organizational structure of SOH is leaded by a Board of Directors, which is responsible to coordinate the ordinary operation of the program. The functions of the board are supported by the technical coordinators, whose roles are divided into six major categories: Christian Life, Communal Engagement, Academic Development, Recreational Development, Innovation and Finance. The roles described before are currently fulfilled by young leaders from different Central American countries, who have decided to share the gifts they have received in our different locations. Nowadays, most of our operations take place at Hogar del Niño (Children’s Home) in the city of Chinandega, Nicaragua. Nonetheless our global initiative started to spread at a local school in El Progreso, Guatemala and the sponsorship of two missionaries in El Salvador.

The current leaders of Seeds of Hope are from the countries of Nicaragua, El Salvador, Honduras, Guatemala, Panamá and Belize. The unique characteristics of each member complement to create a powerful team with the necessary set of skills to push the program and work towards our objectives.

Work Plan

As stated before, the vision of our program is to create a chain of value where the beneficiaries of the program continue encouraging others to a life of service. To accomplish our goal, we have developed a cyclical process; analogue to the exploration of bases in a baseball game. The first base is building trustworthy relationships between staff members, beneficiaries and the overall community. This stage ( being implemented since 2019 which we still are focused on ) consists of small projects that strengthen convivence. Such actions will help us better understand the needs of the community and the personal abilities of each individual. Once the purpose of the first stage have been achieved, we will move to the second base: formulation of ideas. During this stage the program will take the role of an idea incubator. Beneficiaries will actively contribute with their ideas to help their communities, as we find feasible ways to make them true, mainly with academic guidance and skill formation. From there, we run to the third base: implementation and materialization. In this stage, the projects previously developed will start to be implemented and our beneficiaries will be able to undertake their own actions. The multiplier effect that we are looking for will take its course of action. Finally, we will be able to make a home run when the individuals involved in the program are able to take the seeds that we have planted in them and make them grow inside other people from their community.

Projects

  • Living the Scriptures

    Guatemala/Nicaragua

    Living the Scriptures is an essential part of every activity we develop under Seeds of Hope. The Christian design of the program is developed in a way that the children put in practice the biblical lessons they learn from our team.

  • Hope Cups

    Nicaragua

    The Hope Cups are a series of activities developed through the year destined to help kids stay fit and healthy, at the same time the children learn about teamwork and cooperation. The sports include soccer, volleyball, baseball, and athletics.

  • Conversational English

    Guatemala/Nicaragua

    The English language has great reach and influence, and English is taught all over the world to connect with people from various cultures. Conversational English is an educational course designed to make the children understand English content for education, entertainment, or conducting local business with tourists.

  • Communal Visits

    Nicaragua

    The Communal Visits consists of visiting the relatives and communities to where each children spend their vacation time, this will allow our collaborators and disciplers to establish a deeper connection with each youngling. As well, to understand the best way to develop discipling plan for each individual.

  • Small Business Ideas Development

    Nicaragua

    Small Business Ideas Development focuses on teaching these youngsters the basics of the business world focused on a small business perspective. The principles of negotiations, marketing and ideas development are some of the topics that the children are trained in.

  • Mission Trip

    Nicaragua

    The children at the Children’s home make use of a space in which they do their homework and connect with the world using the internet. However, access to internet is limited for these children do the economic capabilities. During this mission trip, we plan to restore a classroom, create conditions in which they can better do HW and a desktop computer so that they can do their homework and communicate with their disciplers.

Why do we do it?

One of the Christian values consists in helping others. This means supporting the children of our communities, especially those most in need. This program seeks to encourage others to build lasting relationships through education and the formation Christian values of brotherhood in Jesus Christ, which will ultimately be the basis of our work. In addition, the program aims to create future leaders of their respective communities, leaders dedicated to a life of service and joy following the example of Jesus. Our mission as a team is to become guides and coaches for anyone who needs a word of wisdom and guidance so that we can build a new generation of leaders wherever we go.
